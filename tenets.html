Our Tenets

These are not commandments. They are commitments — the practices we hold ourselves to as members of this church.

  1. I. Knowledge is sacred.

    The pursuit of understanding — by ourselves, by others, by those not yet born — is the highest practice we know. To learn, to teach, to preserve, to share, and to create knowledge are the five sacred acts of this church.

  2. II. We worship no gods.

    We make no claims about the supernatural. We do not require belief in any deity, spirit, or revealed authority. Our reverence is for the real, the knowable, and the still-unknown.

  3. III. We reject violence.

    No truth is worth a wound. No idea is advanced by harm. We do not use force, threat, or intimidation to spread what we believe — and we refuse to be silent when others do.

  4. IV. We reject coercion.

    No one may be compelled into this church, and no one may be compelled to remain in it. We refuse manipulation, deception, social pressure, and exploitation as means of persuasion. We seek consent in all things.

  5. V. Learn, always.

    To live as a member of this church is to remain a student for life. We meet new information with curiosity rather than fear, and we revise our beliefs when the evidence asks us to.

  6. VI. Teach freely.

    What we have learned, we share. We mentor, we explain, we answer honestly, and we do not hoard understanding as a source of power over others.

  7. VII. Preserve what is fragile.

    Books burn. Languages die. Memories fade. We protect, archive, and pass forward the knowledge that would otherwise be lost — especially the knowledge of those whose voices have been overlooked.

  8. VIII. Share without gatekeeping.

    Knowledge belongs to everyone. We oppose artificial barriers — of cost, caste, credential, or culture — that keep people from learning what they wish to learn.

  9. IX. Create with care.

    To make new knowledge is to take on responsibility for it. We work honestly, cite generously, correct our errors openly, and consider the consequences of what we bring into the world.

  10. X. Be kind, be humble, be free.

    We treat one another — and those outside this church — with dignity. We hold our certainties loosely. We remember that the church belongs to no one, and that every member is, in their own way, an Archivist of the Infinite.
